In this spiritual desert, I see our weakness and our tendency to choose mammon over Christ. The core of our failure and the world's apostasy is not external events, but an internal crisis: we doubt God. Like the Israelites in the desert, we question Him when we face thirst and hardship. This doubt is the root of every betrayal—it hardens our hearts, leads to idolatry, and chokes our love. We see it in personal tragedies, in fear, in complaints, and in demanding guarantees from God instead of trusting Him. Christ overcame these temptations. We must do the same by rejecting doubt, bearing our crosses with patience, and loving Him unconditionally. This is the narrow path to remaining loyal sons and daughters of Christ.
