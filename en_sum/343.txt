As we prepare for Great Lent, I want to focus on a crucial spiritual task: understanding the true nature of evil. Many, even among believers, misunderstand this. They ask, "Why focus on evil, sin, and the devil? Why not just talk about love and positivity?"

The answer is that Scripture and the entire life of the Church are intensely focused on this battle. From the Fall of Adam and Eve to the prophets, the Book of Job, Christ's temptation, and the Lord's Prayer—which teaches us to pray for deliverance from the evil one—the reality of evil is central. Church history, the writings of the Holy Fathers, and the lives of the ascetics all center on the fight against the devil, passions, and sin.

So why is this so vital? Because the ultimate evil we face is not primarily external systems, digital controls, or political agendas. Those are just tools. The real battle is within the human heart. It is the immense, deceptive power that pulls each of us toward sin, betrayal of Christ, and spiritual sleep. We live in a spell of consumerism, entertainment, and empty routine, devoid of true spiritual life, often without even realizing it.

We must first remove the "beam" from our own eye—the inner enemy. The coming Antichrist will not conquer through technology alone; he will seek to win our hearts and souls. You can have perfectly orthodox beliefs in your mind, yet in your heart be a slave to mammon. This is why true spirituality, prayer, and hesychasm are essential: they expose the state of our heart, revealing to whom it truly belongs.

Judas Iscariot is a typos (a type) of the Antichrist. His betrayal was hated by God, yet it occurred and was used to bring about God's glory. This is a profound paradox. In our own lives, every sin is a betrayal of God—whether it's perversion, corruption, consumerism, or idle distraction. It is choosing an illusory world over communion with our Creator.

The great danger today is forgetting the real God—the God of Scripture and the ascetic saints—and replacing Him with a fantasy that doesn't fit reality. As we approach Great Lent, our task is clear: to know this real God, to love Him, and to fight the inner battle. We fast and pray to seek Him alone, to conquer the enemy within that deceives us and steals our salvation. May God strengthen us on this path.
