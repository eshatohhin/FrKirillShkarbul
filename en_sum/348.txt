The ultimate purpose of the Great Lent is not about following rules or dietary restrictions. It is about love for Christ—a radical, personal relationship where we allow Him to change our hearts, our thoughts, and our lives. Fasting is not an end in itself; it is a means to draw nearer to Him, to break away from the world and our old selves. True spiritual struggle is not about what we eat or avoid; it is about vigilance, standing guard over our hearts, and being ready to face real spiritual warfare with Christ at the center. Let this Lent be a celebration of seeking Him, serving others, and offering our whole lives to God. Only then does fasting become alive and lead us to salvation.
