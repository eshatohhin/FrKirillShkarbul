As we enter Great Lent on this Forgiveness Sunday, I call us to examine our hearts. True fasting is not merely following dietary rules or checking boxes—that is the mindset of Cain, whose sacrifice God rejected. Nor is it about mindless conformity, going through the motions like everyone else. Both approaches are empty.

Fasting is a daring act of free love—a conscious, personal pursuit of God and His Kingdom. It is a time to fundamentally change who we are, to break our old selves with a spiritual hammer. We must set real goals: deep prayer, reading Scripture and holy books, meaningful labor, and guarding our speech and senses from noise and distraction.

Beware of modern fallacies that corrupt the fast: treating it as a culinary challenge to make "tasty" fasting food, making exceptions like seafood, or letting one failure convince you to abandon the effort entirely. The fast is meant to humble us, to let us feel hunger, and to confront our deepest passions—be it anger, greed, vanity, or gluttony.

Identify that one thing that keeps you from Christ. This Lent is your chance, with God's grace, to overcome it. We have only these six weeks for grief, repentance, and ascetic struggle, to prepare our hearts for Holy Week and the joy of Pascha.

Do not let your fast be rejected. Let it be a true sacrifice of love, a journey back to God. May this Lent be a time of genuine repentance, healing, and resurrection for our souls.
