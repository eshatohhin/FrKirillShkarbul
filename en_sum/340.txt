I wish you all a Happy New Year and thank God for His blessings in the past year, asking for His grace in 2026. As Christians, we must reflect on the meaning of the New Year. The issue isn’t celebrating it, but why it’s celebrated on January 1st—a date rooted in secular, human-centered traditions rather than God’s design. This shift reflects a deeper spiritual problem: the world’s calendar seeks to reprogram our mindset, aligning us with a progressive, humanistic worldview that distances us from God’s cyclical understanding of time centered on salvation history, like the Church’s tradition of beginning the year in September.

Even Christmas celebrations have been distorted, often carrying a spirit contrary to Christ—focusing on superficial joy rather than repentance and truth. Traditions like Christmas trees, originally Christian symbols of life and hope in Christ, have been repurposed by secular forces, especially in places like Russia under Stalin, to erase true faith. We must stay vigilant, embracing our call to be in the world but not of it, rejecting deceitful influences and holding fast to our faith. May God grant us wisdom and humility to see through these deceptions. Amen.
