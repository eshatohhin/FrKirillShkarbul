Christ is risen! The feast of Pascha is the center of our faith, celebrating not just the resurrection but Christ's entire sacrifice—from His humble birth to His glorious ascension. This sacrifice is radically different from all others.

Pagan sacrifices were a transactional exchange with gods understood by human logic. Old Testament Jewish sacrifices, given by God for purification, were shadows that had to be repeated. Christ's sacrifice is the eternal reality: God Himself offering Himself once and for all. To the world, this is madness or a stumbling block.

True Christianity is not about rituals or ideas, but participating in this Paschal mystery. It means first repenting and humbling ourselves, then dedicating our entire lives to God. This leads to taking up our own cross—embracing obedience to God's will through inevitable sufferings, losses, and sacrifices. Only by walking this path with Christ can we share in His resurrection, be perfected, and ultimately be glorified with Him.

This Pascha, let us ask: where are we on this path? Our focus must be on uniting ourselves to Christ's sacrifice, not on mere celebration. Let us throw off everything that hinders us, so we may live in Him and He in us, entering into the joy and mystery of His resurrection.
