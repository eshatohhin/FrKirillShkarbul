As we enter Passion Week, I want us to truly grasp the meaning of the Cross. It’s not enough to simply believe—true Christianity requires taking up our cross and following Christ, dying to our selfishness, sins, and attachments to this world. Just as a seed must die to bear fruit, we must die to ourselves to be reborn in Him.

Look at the attitudes around Christ’s crucifixion: some wanted Him gone to return to “normal life,” others resisted His suffering. But Christ embraced the Cross—that was His purpose. Our attitude during Passion Week should mirror His: not counting down the days until we can indulge again, but embracing this time as a small reflection of our entire calling—sacrifice for the sake of Christ.

This same principle applies to the times we live in. The world is moving toward deception, control, and apostasy. Many just want to be left alone to their earthly lives, but as Christians, we must see with spiritual eyes. The growing darkness is a sign—our Golgotha is approaching, and with it, the promise of resurrection.

Let’s adopt the mindset of Christ in His final days. Knowing His death was near, He was fearless and outspoken, wholly focused on His mission. If we live with that same urgency—detached from worldly goals, vigilant in prayer, and ready to be living sacrifices—we will understand what truly matters.

May the Lord give us the courage to take up our cross, the clarity to see through the world’s deception, and the hope of resurrection life. Amen.
