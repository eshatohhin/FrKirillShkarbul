We are in the midst of Great Lent, a time to reflect on the Lord’s coming and the final judgment. Many think of Christ’s first coming in the flesh and await a distant second coming on the clouds. But the Holy Fathers teach us that His second coming is happening now—in the Church. At Pentecost, Christ united with His disciples in Spirit, and now He walks in the world through His Body, the Church. This is the ongoing reality of the Gospel today.

Yet we often live as though we’re merely waiting. We reduce faith to study or ritual, losing sight of Christ living in us. Like ancient Israel, we can break covenant with God and slip into apostasy, even within the Church. This weakens our witness. The world is now being tested—every person must encounter Christ and choose. This is why the Gospel must reach all nations before the end.

We face a spiritual battle. The devil fosters enmity, pretending all is well, then persecutes the Church when he can. He uses worldly systems to oppose God’s work. But his plans are not hidden—we are called to disrupt them, like the priests at Jericho, through prayer, mission, and faithful living. Though true followers of Christ are few, as they were in His earthly ministry, we are blessed to partake in His presence now, more than the apostles before Pentecost.

This Lent, let us repent and cling to Christ. Let us love the cross, reject worldliness, and join Christ in His ongoing coming—writing the Gospel today through our lives. May we find joy and courage to follow Him to Golgotha and witness His love to all.
