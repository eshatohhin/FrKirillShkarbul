In times of rapid change and crisis—like today's revolutions in technology, society, and values—many Christians react with fear, seeing only decline and moral collapse. But history shows us that every great upheaval is also a divine opportunity for the Church to grow and bear fruit.

When Christ came, it was an era of deep crisis: philosophies were bankrupt, worship was corrupt, and people were lost in skepticism and sin. Yet, precisely in that turmoil, the Gospel took root—transforming marginalized lives and eventually reaching half the world. Similarly, during plagues in the Roman Empire, while others fled, Christians stayed to care for the sick, revealing the true face of faith and drawing multitudes to Christ.

We see this pattern repeated: in the conversion of the Slavs, in the successful missions of St. Nicholas of Japan amid social collapse, and in the spiritual awakening that followed Russia’s perestroika. When old systems fail and comfort zones shatter, hearts become open to truth.

So now, as we face AI, social fragmentation, and global uncertainty, we must not retreat into fear or complaint. Instead, we should be wise and strong in faith, ready to testify to Christ’s love. The devil may stir chaos, but God allows these trials to shake people from spiritual sleep—like throwing someone into water to teach them to swim. Yes, many may drown in confusion, but some will awaken and find salvation.

Our task is not to mourn a lost world but to courageously step forward, breaking free from mere tradition and comfort. We must allow Lent and life’s trials to burn away our old selves, embracing sacrifice and trusting fully in God. Providence will not act without our courage. Let our hearts be eager for His work, ready to bear fruit that saves both us and those around us.
